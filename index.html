Introduction:

Hello my name is Samantha and today I'd like to introduce some of my favorite bands, genres, and favorite songs. Music has been around for thousands of years, and it's evolved over time. Before we had our own language, we made simple instruments likely made out of bones and other materials. Even though we hadn't develop a structured language yet, we may have used our voices to express our emotions and feelings. I'd like to imagine humans were always expressive and creative in their own way. Bringing us back to the current times, there are so many genres to explore. Some are mainstream like metal, rap, and, pop. Others are a bit more niche, like glitchcore, shoegaze, and nu-metal. If it's okay, I'd like to introduce some bands and genres that resonate with me. Without further ado, let's get into it!

Glitchcore:

According to Aesthetic Wiki, "Glitchcore as a 2020s music genre is inspired by the dance music vibes of the mid to late 2000s and the sped-up aesthetics of early 2010s nightcore." This genre is one of my favorite genres to listen to. It often has a lot going on within their songs. There's a lot of unfamiliar instruments, often with a beat to it. Then there's the vocals, they often sound ethereal and otherwordly. Yet somehow some songs bring a slight melancholic feeling to them. A good example of this would be 4 Morant (better luck next time) - Doja Cat (explicit). On the other hand, glitchcore can also feel like you're traveling through a galaxy. A song I like that represents this would be Suffocation - Crystal Castles. I hope these two examples gave a good idea of what glitchcore exactly is! Moving onto the next genre is shoegaze!

Glitchcore Artists:

  • Crystal Castles
  • Odetari (often explicit)
  • Marluxiam

Shoegaze:

Continuing onto shoegaze, it is also one of my favorite genres. In my opinion, shoegaze focuses more on the soft voices, mixed with heavier rock instrumentals added in. Referencing Aesthetic Wiki again, "Shoegaze is a genre of alternative rock that originated in the late 80s. The genre is characterized by its shimmering vocals, reverberating guitars, and textural distortion...""Shoegaze is a genre of alternative rock that originated in the late 80s. The genre is characterized by its shimmering vocals, reverberating guitars, and textural distortion...". A song that strongly represents this genre to a T is Your Face - Wisp. This music makes me feel less alone, where my thoughts are loud and my hope is none. I feel understood when I hear this type of music. It feels like a defeated, sad anger. Maybe acceptance of a situation. A Sad Cartoon - Loathe is another song that I deeply resonate with. While I struggle to interpret the lyrics and create my own meaning from it, the feelings I get when I listen to this song while driving is like no other. I hope you can appreciate this song as much as I do.

Shoegaze Artists:

  • Mareux
  • Loathe
  • my bloody valentine

Nu-Metal:

Nu-metal is described as "often playing on pure shock factor or raw emotion with songs about... otherwise controversial subjects", from Aesthetic Wiki as well. Lately, I've more aligned with nu-metal compared to the other genres. I like it's heavy vocals and guitar rich music. Some nu-metal music can lean less on the original metal genre and instead add in a more transcendant feeling, a bit like shoegaze. Cherry Waves - Deftones handles this example very well and has been one of my favorite songs for many years. If we want to explore a more true to genre song that represents nu-metal, then the song Numb - Linkin Park is a great example. In this song he really expresses his feeling of depression and inferiority. While some may view it as controversial, I now see it as a cry for help that many listeners missed. I'd like to touch more on this artist in the next topic.

Nu-Metal Artists:

  • Linkin Park
  • Deftones
  • System of a Down

Linkin Park and Chester Bennington:

While I don't know much about the entire band, I do know they created many top hit songs such as "One Step Closer", "Crawling", and "In The End". Chester, born in Phoenix, Arizona, March 20, 1976, died on July 20, 2017. He was the lead vocalist within the band. He often struggled with depression and substance abuse for the majority of his life. From ages 7-13, he was abused inappropriately by an older male friend. Despite what he went through, he was a beacon of hope for millions of people who felt just like him - depressed, suicidal, and lost. Chester will always have a special place in my heart, and my heart goes out to the millions of people who loved him just as much as I do. Even though he is no longer with us, he is still so prevalent in many people's lives today. That is why I will always respect and love their band, and if you haven't listened to any of his music, I'd highly recommend it.

Deftones:

Deftones might be one of my top bands ever. The band created in Sacramento, California, formed in 1988 consists of 5 members. Chino Moreno (frontman), Stephen Carptenter (lead guitarist), Abe Cunningham (drummer), Chi Cheng (bassist), and Frank Delgado (keyboardist and turntablist). To me, Deftone's ability to create such diverse music is like no other. They've contributed to so many subgenres of rock and metal. Whenever I hear the band play, I'm immersed and I feel all of the emotions they convey. It's my dream to one day to meet them in real life and hear them play live. I know many of their songs by heart and I've been a fan for years. They've inspired me so much and are often the reason for my motivation to keep on pushing through my challenges.
